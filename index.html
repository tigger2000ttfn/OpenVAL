Origin · The Hidden Reference
Two of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World — hidden inside a single mark.

The Pharos of Alexandria stood for over a thousand years as the tallest man-made structure on Earth. A lighthouse so monumental it became the ancient word for lighthouse in a dozen languages. It didn't guide Greek ships, or Roman ships, or Egyptian ships. It guided every ship — through the same dangerous waters, regardless of what they carried or where they were bound.

The name PHAROLON carries that lighthouse inside it: PHAROS + LON. The ancient beacon, extended forward. A platform built to last not for one regulatory era, but for every era that follows.

And the icon? Look closer. The pyramid inside the compass ring is no coincidence. The Great Pyramid of Giza — another of the Seven Wonders, standing on the same soil as the Pharos — is encoded into the mark. The nested triangles. The ancient stone courses. The apex that catches the light. Two wonders. One symbol.
